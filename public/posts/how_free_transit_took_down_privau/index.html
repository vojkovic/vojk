How free transit in took down my website

November 1, 2024 2-minute read
Networking • Bgp

How free transit in took down my website Link to heading

I run a small anycast network for priv.au, a metasearch engine. This network is built on a few servers around the world, each connected to different Internet Exchange Points (IXPs) and transit providers.

Anycast is a routing technique that allows multiple servers to share the same IP address. When a user sends a request to that IP, the network routes it to the closest server. This improves performance and resilience by spreading the load across multiple locations.

The Setup Link to heading

I introduced a new PoP in Canada and got a port to ONIX (a Toronto IX) through my hosting provider. The server wasn’t anything too fancy - just a tiny server to help with local routing in North America. I connected it to ONIX, and shortly after Hurricane Electric, reached out to offer free transit and established a BGP session with me.

What happened next? Disaster.

What Went Wrong Link to heading

Once the BGP session went live, the VPS started drowning in traffic. All the trafic destined for my prefix was being sent to this VPS in Canada.

Why did this happen?

AS_PATH selection.

Because Hurricane Electric is a Tier 1 provider and peers with almost everyone who asks, their AS_PATH was shorter than any other path to my prefix. This made my VPS the best route to reach my prefix, and the traffic started pouring in.

Diagram:

The VPS didn’t stand a chance. It’s resources were completely overwhelmed - I’d turned my network into a single point of failure.

Fixing the Mess Link to heading

Here’s how I got things back on track:

  1. Immediately Disabled the BGP Session: I immediately shut down the BGP session with HE on the VPS to stop the flood of traffic.

The Takeaway Link to heading

This experience taught me that even small changes to a BGP setup can have massive consequences. Free transit from Hurricane Electric is an amazing resource, but it needs to be used carefully—especially in an anycast setup.
